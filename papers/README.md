# Downloaded Papers

This directory contains the key papers identified for the research topic: "Do Multi-Turn Conversations Regress to the Prior?"

| File | Title | Authors | Year | Relevance |
|------|-------|---------|------|-----------|
| `2512.12775_Persistent_Personas.pdf` | Persistent Personas? Role-Playing, Instruction Following, and Safety in Extended Interactions | Araujo et al. | 2025 | Foundational study on persona degradation and reversion to default behavior over 100+ rounds. |
| `2505.06120v1_LLMs_Get_Lost_In_Multi-Turn_Conversation.pdf` | LLMs Get Lost In Multi-Turn Conversation | Laban et al. | 2025 | Defines "Lost in Conversation" (LiC) and quantifies performance and reliability drop in multi-turn settings. |
| `2402.11690v1_Measuring_and_Controlling_Instruction__In_Stability_in_Language_Model_Dialogs.pdf` | Measuring and Controlling Instruction (In)Stability in Language Model Dialogs | Li et al. | 2024 | Analyzes instruction drift and attention decay over conversations. |
| `2510.07777v2_Drift_No_More__Context_Equilibria_in_Multi-Turn_LLM_Interactions.pdf` | Drift No More? Context Equilibria in Multi-Turn LLM Interactions | Dongre et al. | 2025 | Investigates context drift as a bounded stochastic process and proposes equilibria framework. |
| `2503.11656v1_TRUTH_DECAY__Quantifying_Multi-Turn_Sycophancy_in_Language_Models.pdf` | TRUTH DECAY: Quantifying Multi-Turn Sycophancy in Language Models | Liu et al. | 2025 | Examines how biases like sycophancy evolve and persist in extended dialogues. |
| `2505.18541v1_Persistent_Personas__Role-Playing__Instruction_Following__and_Safety_in_Extended_Interactions.pdf` | RoleRAG: Enhancing LLM Role-Playing via Graph Guided Retrieval | Wang et al. | 2025 | Complementary work on improving role-playing consistency (initially misidentified but useful for mitigation context). |
